Business Understanding

Business Objective

Build an optimization model to predict startup profit and identify which department budgets drive profitability for investors.

Key Questions

Q1: Which budget department most impacts company profit?

Q2: Does R&D innovation spending correlate with profit?

Q3: Does marketing spending increase profit?

Q4: Does geographical state location matter?

Success Criteria

R² > 0.90 • Low MAE • Low RMSE • Unbiased Cross-Validation

Collinearity Concern

R&D and Marketing correlation (r ≈ 0.78) requires regularization (L1/L2) to prevent variance inflation.

Modeling & Model Selection

Profit = β₀ + β₁Z(R&D) + β₂Z(Administration) + β₃Z(Marketing) + β₄(State) + ε

Validation Strategy

Compared 10 selection schemes (Hold-out, 5-Fold CV, LOOCV, Nested CV, Bayesian Optimization, AIC/BIC).

Standardization

Scaled features using StandardScaler so coefficients are directly comparable.

Regularization

Lasso Regression (L1) chosen to handle multicollinearity and perform automatic feature selection.

GridSearch Tuning

Exhaustive search on alpha parameter grid: optimal alpha = 1000.0.

Business Insights & Takeaways

Insight 1 (R&D Spend): Primary driver (coeff: +$34,884). R&D innovation yields the highest ROI on profit.
Insight 2 (Marketing Spend): Secondary positive driver (coeff: +$5,554). Marketing campaigns expand margins effectively.
Insight 3 (Administration): Negligible impact (coeff: -$729). Administrative costs do not support profit scaling and should be managed tightly.
Insight 4 (State Location): Geographic registry location (California, Florida, New York) has exactly 0.0 contribution. Focus budgets on operations, not location.
